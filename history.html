History of Houston

The Founding of Houston

In 1836, two brothers, Augustus and John Allen, founded Houston near the banks of Buffalo Bayou. They named the city after Sam Houston, a leader in the Texas Revolution and the first president of the Republic of Texas.

Growth and Industry

Houston's growth was fueled by its role as a hub for trade and transportation. The establishment of the Port of Houston in 1914 turned the city into a critical gateway for shipping and commerce, further bolstered by the discovery of oil in 1901.

Key Historical Milestones

  • 1837: Houston was officially incorporated as a city.
  • 1860s: Houston became a vital logistics hub during the Civil War.
  • 1901: The Spindletop oil discovery began Houston's rise as an energy capital.
  • 1961: NASA’s Johnson Space Center opened, earning Houston the nickname “Space City.”

Modern-Day Houston

Today, Houston is celebrated for its diversity, culture, and innovation. The city boasts a thriving arts scene, world-renowned museums, and a reputation as a global energy and aerospace leader. It’s also home to one of the most diverse populations in the United States, contributing to its vibrant culinary and cultural experiences.
